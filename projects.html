TECH108 - Pyjama Set

The pyjama set includes light pink koala themed long pants, that is paired with a grey cotton v-neck t-shirt to colour match the koalas on the pyjama pants pattern. The eucalyptus leaf in the centre of the t-shirt has been screen-printed on with dark green ink, which is also showcased throughout the pants. The tote-bag has a pocket placed in the centre, which utilises the matching koala patterned fabric, as well as a white button and pink loophole.

TECH108 - E-Textiles Felt Wall Hanging

A felt banner using pieces of commercial wool felt and hand-made felt, to create a mini wall hanging banner that presents a hawaiian theme. Additionally, the banner also has a working electronic soft circuit embdedded into the centre of the flower and surfboard, using conductive threads and small LEDs.

TECH209 - Quilt

This nine patch quilt utilises light and pastel colours, to constrast with the vibrant yellow dyed fabric positioned around the centre of the piece, as well as the dark liquid radiance purple fabric on the outer of the patch design. In drawing inspiration from the Australian print designer, Lisa Gorman, colour contrast has been utilised, in conjunction with floral and botanical symbols screen printed in bold white, reflecting her contradicting and variation in patterns.

TECH211 - Thread Winder

An automatic bobbin and thread winder model. A floss card or bobbin can be inserted into the machine, as well as the thread intended to wind, will spin and wind up, storing the thread onto the card. The model is powered by a switch connected to wall power, in which the user wraps their thread around the card, and waits for the ultrasonic sensor to detect there is an object and will begin its automation.

TECH212 - Medieval Costume - Group Task

A medieval costume is was intended for an ACU student to wear to the on-campus live Medieval history event. The earth toned ensemble, representing a herbalist, consists of a chemise, kirtle, apron and a cape/hood. The ensemble also features a belt, where a satchel connects to it, enhancing the functional aspects of the design allowing for ease of transportation of herbs and herbal potions.
